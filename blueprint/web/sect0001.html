1 Bombieri–Vinogradov

1.1 Statement

The main result is the Bombieri–Vinogradov theorem, which gives a strong estimate for primes in arithmetic progressions on average over moduli.

Lemma 31
#

Uses

  • Lemma 30

Lean declarations

  • character_sum_Mobius

Let \(f\) be an arithmetic function. For \(r \le x\), \(q {\gt} 1\) and \((a, q) = 1\),

\[ \mathop{{\sum \nolimits ^{*}}}_{\xi \pmod{q}} \bar\xi (a) \sum _{n \le y} \xi (n) f_r(n) = \sum _{d \mid q} \mu (q/d)\, \varphi (d)\, \Delta _{f_{rq}}(y;\, d,\, a) \]
Proof ▶

Fix \(P \in \mathbb {N}\) with \(q \mid P\). Define \(F_P\) and \(G_P\) on divisors of \(P\) by

\begin{align*} F_P(q) & := \sum _{\chi \ne \chi _0 \pmod{q}} \bar\chi (a) \sum _{n \le y} \chi (n) f_{rP}(n) = \Delta _{f_{rP}}(y;\, q,\, a), \\ G_P(d) & := \mathop{{\sum \nolimits ^{*}}}_{\xi \pmod{d}} \bar\xi (a) \sum _{n \le y} \xi (n) f_{rP}(n) \quad (d {\gt} 1),\quad G_P(1) = 0. \end{align*}

Since every non-principal character mod \(q\) factors through a unique primitive character, \(F_P(q) = \sum _{d \mid q} G_P(d)\). By Möbius inversion,

\[ G_P(q) = \sum _{d \mid q} \mu (q/d)\, F_P(d) = \sum _{d \mid q} \mu (q/d)\, \Delta _{f_{rP}}(y;\, d,\, a). \]

Set \(P = q\) to conclude.
